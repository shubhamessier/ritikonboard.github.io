My Work

  • Networks Research Lab IIITD

    Research Intern, June 2024 - present

    I am working under the guidance of Dr. Arani Bhattacharya on 'Cloud-Assisted Autonomous Driving over Wireless Network'. The main goal was to achieve minimum network latency in real-time video streaming of the vehicle's perception data and to enable Teleoperation over the cloud network. Integrated CARLA with FFmpeg for efficient encoding and streaming of video data over an RTSP stream, optimizing streaming parameters to minimize latency. Added the teleoperation/manual control feature on the cloud server using keyboard strokes to simulate real-time vehicle control while keeping latency in check. Tested the setup with various video resolutions and network conditions, obtaining significant results. We also worked on modifying PYLOT's Erdos pipeline to enable cloud-based object detection using Yolov8, improving accuracy in complex scenarios. This involved using a lighter detection model on both the server (v8x) and the vehicle (v8n in place of faster-rcnn), with smooth auto-switching between models based on scenarios. Future work involves studying the LIDAR data and working on modifying the rest of Pylot pipeline. Working on this at NRL provided me valuable experience in autonomous vehicle technology, networks, video encoding and related works. Presented our poster titled "Cloud-Assisted Autonomous Driving Over Wireless Network" at ACM COMPASS 2024 held in IIITD between July 8-11th.
